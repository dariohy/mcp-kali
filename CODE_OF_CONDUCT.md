# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We want participation in MCP Kali to be safe, respectful, and productive for
people of every background and experience level. Project spaces include the
repository, issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other channels
managed by the project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Welcome reasonable questions and differing levels of experience.
- Respect privacy, authorization boundaries, and responsible disclosure.
- Use synthetic data when discussing security behavior.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about project scope and safety.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Sharing credentials, private targets, customer data, or live exploit details.
- Encouraging unauthorized access or using project spaces to coordinate abuse.
- Repeated disruption after a maintainer asks for behavior to stop.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through an
established private channel. Do not include sensitive engagement data. The
maintainer may edit or remove content, close interactions, issue a warning, or
temporarily or permanently restrict participation when necessary.

Reports will be handled as privately and promptly as practical. Good-faith
reports and participants who are the subject of a report will be treated
fairly, with decisions based on the available context and project safety.
